Architects Statement:

The summer house is located on the island of Öland in southern Sweden. The island's barren and windswept landscapes with vast fields, low vegetation and limestone formations have inspired the design of the buildings. The limestone walls and the protruding roofs protect against weather and wind and the large glass sections take in the view of the sea and the landscape outside.

The limestone comes from a local stone quarry and has the same color as the surrounding limestone slabs, wood panels on the outside are specially sawn and untreated so that over time they will have the same gray tone as the stone. Inside, the floors are made of cast-in-place concrete and carpentry and wall panels are made of ash.
